\begin{frame}{Astrophysical Reaction rates}
	
\begin{itemize}
	\item Calculate $\sigma_{(\mathrm{n},\gamma)}$ for $^{187}\mathrm{Os}$ and compare it to experimental data for energies relevant in the s-process.
	\item Calculate the MACS ($\langle \sigma v \rangle$) of $^{187}\mathrm{Os}$(n,$\gamma$) for a typical temperature in the s-process and compare to \textit{KADONIS}.
	\item In stellar interiors, it is believe that the target nuclei exists not only in the ground state, but in a thermodynamical equilibrium with its excited states.
	\begin{itemize}
		\item Discuss, briefly, the reasons behind this assumption.
		\item What happens in TALYS by default? Will it assume target nuclei in ground state only?
		\item Compare the MACS with and without treating the target nucleus in ground-state only. Explain the differences. (What were the reasons for doing this?)
		\item Compare the results to information from KADONIS
	\end{itemize} 
\end{itemize}

Hints: You can use the keyword \pythoninline{astroE} followed by $kT$ in MeV to calculate the MACS. (see next page)

\end{frame}
